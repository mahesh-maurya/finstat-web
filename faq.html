Frequently Asked Questions

General FAQs

1. What is the course structure?

The Actuarial Course is divided broadly into 4 sets of exams, namely Core Technical (CT) series, Core Application (CA) series, Specialized Technical (ST) series and Specialized Application (SA) series. In order to become an Actuary one must clear 15 papers along with 3 years of professional working experience.
The breakup of the same is as follows:



  • CT Series: All 9 papers
  • CA Series: All 3 papers
  • ST Series: Any 2 of 8 papers
  • SA Series: Any 1 of 6 papers

The choice in the Specialized Technical and Application series depends on the area of specialization that one wants to pursue in the actuarial domain. For detailed syllabus refer to the IFAâ€™s website here

2. Is there an order in which to appear the papers?

There is no set order to appear papers which is prescribed. A student may appear for any paper depending on his qualification/aptitude. However, CT level exams are needed to be cleared first before giving CA level exams and so on.

3. What educational background is required to pursue this course?

The Institute of Actuaries has not prescribed any particular qualification to pursue the course except for the membership criteria as described by us here. However, the course structure involves a considerable amount of statistical, analytical skills along with financial application of the skills.

4. Can I pursue the course with graduation?

Yes. It is advisable to pursue the course with graduation. You may appear for exams which compliment with the area of your graduation. For example, a student pursuing graduation in commerce stream may appear for CT2 - Finance and Financial Reporting exam while a student from science stream can give CT3 - Probability and Statistics exam, etc.

Attempting an actuarial exam that compliments your graduation studies will achieve the dual goal of clearing actuarial papers along with graduation.

5. What is duration of the course?

There is no prescribed duration of the course. However, the course structure requires excellent skills along with hard work. Single minded devotion, total dedication and a systematic approach to problems are the qualities that will enable a person to qualify as an actuary within a reasonable time and to become a fellow member of the institute. There are following intermittent qualifications: Certificate of Actuarial Techniques (CAT) - Completion of all CT series papers, Associate Member (AIAI) - Completion of all CT and CA series papers and Actuarial Analyst- The composition of the same is under approval. For more details visit this link.

6. Does the course have international recognition?

Actuary is one of the courses which has excellent international recognition with the UK Institute and other member bodies of International Actuarial Association. There is a one to one exemption for all the CT papers. Further, processes to have an agreement at full Fellowship level are in place.

7. When are the exams conducted?

The exams are held twice a year; for the Indian Institute they are in May and November and of the UK Institute are held on April and September. The exams are conducted in centers across India.

However, it is always advisable to book a seat in your nearest center.

8. Are there any restrictions on the number of attempts?

Neither there are any restrictions on number of attempts nor there is any time limit within which the exams are to be completed.

9. Is there any eligibility criterion for appearing exams?

There is an entrance exam of ACET for Indian Institute and CT1 Non- Members for the UK Institute. Clearance of the above mentioned exams makes one eligible for the membership of the Indian/ UK Institute.

ACET FAQs


1. What is the eligibility criterion for appearing ACET?

The only criterion is that the examinee must have appeared or cleared 10+2 examination or equivalent examinations with English as a subject in the same. There is no exemption from appearing ACET even though an individual is a professional like Chartered Accountant, Cost Accountant or Company Secretary or possess any other qualification.

2. What is the exam pattern?

The exam is a multiple choice questions based with negative markings. It comprises of 55 questions totaling to 100 marks. The breakup of questions is as follows:



  • 25 questions: 1 marks each
  • 25 questions: 2 marks each
  • 05 questions: 5 marks each

There is a 50% negative marking for each wrong answer. For instance, if a sum carrying 5 marks goes wrong, 2.5 marks will be deducted for that question.
